Why Parley

A meeting note platform you can shape around the job

Most meeting tools ship a fixed note format and a fixed model. Parley is built for teams that need their own prompts, their own providers, and their own definition of what matters.

Own the transcript

Capture both sides of the call, keep speaker labels editable, and turn the conversation into a record you can inspect.

Customize the playbook

Build evaluation templates for negotiation risk, sales qualification, missed objections, follow-ups, or anything your workflow requires.

Ask against the evidence

Ask what happened, what was missed, what to push on next, or what changed. Answers stay grounded in the transcript.

Bring your own providers

Use the transcription vendor and model provider that fit your cost, privacy, language, and latency needs.

Workflow

From call to decision support

Three steps from a live conversation to insight you can act on — without changing how you run the meeting.

  1. 01

    Choose your stack

    Pick the STT provider and model provider you want. Parley stays the meeting layer, not another closed AI bundle.

  2. 02

    Load a playbook

    Use templates for sales, negotiation, hiring, or diligence, then edit the prompts until they match your process.

  3. 03

    Run the meeting

    Watch transcript, questions, flags, and follow-ups update while you stay focused on the person in front of you.

Built-in MCP server

Ask the meeting from your own AI

Parley runs a local Model Context Protocol server. Point Claude — or any MCP client — at it and query the live call as it happens, with the AI app you already use.

Model Context Protocol

Your AI, wired into the live call

No extra key inside Parley — connect the assistant you already pay for and let it read the running transcript, then ask "what did they commit to?" or "what haven't I asked yet?" grounded in the meeting.

  • Reads the live, speaker-labelled transcript
  • Can drive the agenda, evaluations, and timeline
  • Works with Claude, Cursor, and any MCP client
# Connect your MCP client — keep Parley open
claude mcp add --transport http parley \
  http://127.0.0.1:3011/mcp

The exact endpoint (and a ready-to-paste command) lives in Settings → MCP Server.
